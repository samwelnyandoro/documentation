## **Video Structure**

---

### **[INTRO — 20–30 seconds]**

- Briefly introduce yourself and the assignment  
- State the dataset:  
  **University Students Performance & Study Habits 2026 (Kaggle)**  
- Mention the goal:  
  Exploring factors influencing student academic performance  

---

### **[DATASET OVERVIEW — 30–40 seconds]**

- Show the Kaggle page briefly  
- Mention:
  - 10,000 records  
  - 15+ attributes  
  - Mix of numerical and categorical variables  
- Highlight presence of **missing values** (required for task)

---

### **[LOADING & VERIFYING DATA — 30–40 seconds]**

- Run notebook cells live:
  - Show `head()` / `tail()` output  
- Explain `df.info()`:
  - Data types  
  - Non-null counts  
- Show missing values summary  

---

### **[VARIABLE SELECTION — 20–30 seconds]**

- Explain:
  - Dropped columns (IDs, high-missingness)  
- Mention retained variables and why they matter for analysis  

---

### **[EDA — 60–75 seconds]**

- Show descriptive statistics:
  - Highlight key patterns (e.g. skewness)  

- Walk through:
  - 1–2 histograms → explain distributions  
  - Categorical frequency tables → note imbalances  

---

### **[CORRELATION ANALYSIS — 45–60 seconds]**

- Display correlation heatmap  
- Explain:
  - Colour meaning (positive vs negative)  

- Highlight:
  - 2–3 strongest relationships (e.g. study hours vs GPA)  
  - Any weak or surprising correlations  

---

### **[GROUP-BASED CORRELATION — 30–40 seconds]**

- Show segmented heatmaps:
  - By gender or field of study  

- Point out:
  - At least one key difference across groups  
  - Example: stronger stress–GPA relationship in female students  

---

### **[CLOSING — 20–30 seconds]**

- Summarise **2–3 key insights**  
- Give a short closing/sign-off  

---

## **Total Time**
**4–5 minutes** at a comfortable speaking pace  

 *Tip:* Run the notebook before recording so outputs are already rendered — this avoids delays and keeps your narration smooth.
